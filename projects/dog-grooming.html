Dog Grooming
Application

An object-oriented model of a pet grooming business, designed to analyze appointments and both explicit and implicit income. Built around clean OOP principles, with class diagrams, controllers, and sequence diagrams driving the architecture.

Year

Role

Type
System design / OOP
Status
Completed

Overview

The brief was to model a real business — a pet grooming shop — as software. That meant looking past the surface of "appointments and payments" and digging into the underlying transactions, including the implicit income that comes from things like product sales and recurring customers.

The solution used object-oriented programming to give each piece of the business its own class, controllers to orchestrate the logic, and UML diagrams to make sure the structure actually held up before any code was written.

Features

  • Class diagram modeling each type of transaction the shop handles.
  • Controllers managing the application's flow and business logic.
  • Sequence diagrams mapping how problems are solved end-to-end.
  • Analytical breakdown of appointments and revenue streams.
  • Clean OOP architecture, designed to be extended for new features.

Tech Stack

OOP UML Class Diagrams Sequence Diagrams Controllers

This project taught me to think in systems before reaching for code. UML diagrams aren't busywork — they're how you catch design flaws before they become bugs. It's a habit I carry into every project now.
